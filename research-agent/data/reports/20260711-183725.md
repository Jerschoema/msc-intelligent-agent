## Synthesis

This paper [1] demonstrates the feasibility of an 8-week mindfulness meditation program for adolescents with ADHD, showing positive outcomes in symptom reduction and cognitive function. However, the specific focus on adolescents with ADHD limits the generalizability to broader adolescent anxiety populations.

**Key takeaways:**
- Feasibility and satisfaction with 8-week mindfulness training for adolescents with ADHD
- Improvements in self-reported ADHD symptoms, attention, cognitive inhibition, and anxiety/depressive symptoms

**Open questions:**
- How does mindfulness training impact adolescents without ADHD who experience anxiety?
- What is the optimal duration and frequency for mindfulness interventions in adolescent anxiety?
- Are the observed improvements sustained over time?
- Do mindfulness interventions require individualized approaches for different anxiety subtypes?

### 1. Mindfulness Meditation Training in Adults and Adolescents With ADHD
*Lidia Zylowska, Deborah L. Ackerman, May Yang, Julie L. Futrell, Nancy L. Horton, T. Sigi Hale, Caroly Pataki, Susan L. Smalley (2007)*

_Source reputation: unknown — Journal of Attention Disorders_

_Based on the abstract only — the full text was not accessible._

This study assessed the feasibility of an 8-week mindfulness meditation program for adults and adolescents with ADHD. The results indicated that most participants completed the training and reported high satisfaction, with improvements in self-reported ADHD symptoms, attention and cognitive inhibition tasks, as well as anxiety and depressive symptoms. The authors conclude that mindfulness training is feasible for this population and may improve behavioral and neuroc. impairments, suggesting the need for a controlled clinical trial.

**Key claims:**
- Most participants completed the 8-week mindfulness training and reported high satisfaction.
- Pre-post improvements in self-reported ADHD symptoms were observed.
- Improvements in attention and cognitive inhibition tasks were noted.
- Improvements in anxiety and depressive symptoms were observed.

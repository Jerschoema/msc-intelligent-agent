## Findings

### 1. Robustness via Retrying: Closed-Loop Robotic Manipulation with Self-Supervised Learning
*Frederik Ebert, Sudeep Dasari, Alex X. Lee, Sergey Levine, Chelsea Finn (2018)*

The paper introduces a method for training robots to perform complex manipulation tasks using self-supervised learning from raw visual inputs. It proposes a video-prediction based controller that enables continuous retrying of tasks without losing track of the objective, allowing the robot to learn skills like grasping and repositioning objects. The approach uses image registration to maintain object tracking during trials and demonstrates successful performance with only 160 hours of autonomously collected unlabeled data.

**Key claims:**
- An imperfect model can complete complex tasks if it can continuously retry but must not lose track of the objective (p. 1)
- The method uses image registration to keep track of objects of interest throughout the trial (p. 1)
- The approach combines video-prediction based controller with image registration to enable complex behaviors including grasping and non-prehensile manipulation (p. 6)
- A model trained with 160 robot hours of autonomously collected data successfully performs complex manipulation tasks with novel objects (p. 1)

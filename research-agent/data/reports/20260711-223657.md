## Findings

### 1. Dual Accuracy-Quality-Driven Neural Network for Prediction Interval Generation
*Giorgio Morales, John W. Sheppard (2022)*

_Source reputation: medium — Open-access preprint server; dominant venue for ML/physics/CS research but not peer-reviewed_

_Based on the abstract only — the full text was not accessible._

The paper introduces a dual neural network approach to generate prediction intervals (PIs) for regression tasks, emphasizing accuracy and quality. It proposes two companion networks: one for target estimation and another for PI bounds, with a novel loss function balancing interval width minimization and coverage probability. The method achieves narrower PIs while maintaining high coverage and target accuracy across synthetic, benchmark, and real-world crop datasets compared to state-of-the-art methods.

**Key claims:**
- The paper presents a method to learn prediction intervals for regression-based neural networks automatically in addition to the conventional target predictions.
- The main contribution is the design of a novel loss function for the PI-generation network that takes into account the output of the target-estimation network and has two optimization objectives: minimizing the mean prediction interval width and ensuring the PI integrity using constraints that maximize the prediction interval probability coverage implicitly.
- The method achieves significantly narrower PIs without detriment to its target estimation accuracy when compared to three state-of-the-art neural-network-based methods.
- The method was shown to produce higher-quality PIs by maintaining a nominal probability coverage across synthetic, benchmark, and real-world crop datasets.

### 2. The Deep Arbitrary Polynomial Chaos Neural Network or how Deep Artificial Neural Networks could benefit from Data-Driven Homogeneous Chaos Theory
*Sergey Oladyshkin, Timothy Praditia, Ilja Kröker, Farid Mohammadi, Wolfgang Nowak, Sebastian Otte (2023)*

_Source reputation: medium — Open-access preprint server; dominant venue for ML/physics/CS research but not peer-reviewed_

_Based on the abstract only — the full text was not accessible._

This paper proposes a novel approach to enhance Deep Artificial Neural Networks (DANNs) by integrating data-driven homogeneous chaos theory, specifically arbitrary polynomial chaos (aPC), to address limitations in the current neural signal processing framework. The authors argue that conventional DANNs implicitly assume a Gaussian distribution of neural signals and lack orthogonality conditions, leading to redundant representations. By adopting aPC, the paper aims to construct multi-variate orthonormal representations for each DANN node, thereby improving efficiency and accuracy in neural signal processing without altering the DANN's multi-layer architecture or activation functions.

**Key claims:**
- Conventional DANNs implicitly assume a Gaussian distribution of neural signals.
- Conventional DANNs lack orthogonality conditions for neural signals in data-driven applications.
- The current DANN structure relies on a linear superposition of neural activity with non-linearity handled by activation functions.
- Deep arbitrary polynomial chaos neural networks use multi-variate orthonormal representations to improve neural signal processing efficiency.

### 3. Crystal Graph Convolutional Neural Networks for an Accurate and Interpretable Prediction of Material Properties
*Tian Xie, Jeffrey C. Grossman (2018)*

_Source reputation: high — High-impact peer-reviewed journal in physics_

_Based on the abstract only — the full text was not accessible._

This paper introduces a crystal graph convolutional neural network (CGCNN) framework that directly learns material properties from atomic connections in crystals, eliminating the need for manually constructed feature vectors or complex coordinate transformations. The method achieves highly accurate predictions of eight different density functional theory-calculated properties across diverse crystal structures and compositions using only 10^4 training data points. The framework provides interpretable insights by linking local chemical environments to global material properties, as demonstrated through perovskite examples where empirical design rules were derived.

**Key claims:**
- The method directly learns material properties from atomic connections in crystals without requiring manual feature construction or complex coordinate transformations.
- The framework achieves highly accurate predictions of eight density functional theory-calculated properties across diverse crystal structures and compositions using 10^4 training data points.
- The framework provides interpretable insights by linking local chemical environments to global material properties.
- The method demonstrates empirical design rules for perovskites by extracting contributions from local chemical environments to global properties.

### 4. A compact review of molecular property prediction with graph neural networks
*Oliver Wieder, Stefan M. Kohlbacher, Mélaine A. Kuenemann, Arthur Garon, Ducrot Pierre, Thomas Seidel, Thierry Langer (2020)*

_Source reputation: unknown — Drug Discovery Today Technologies_

_Based on the abstract only — the full text was not accessible._

This review provides a structured analysis of graph neural networks (GNNs) applied to molecular property prediction in drug discovery. The authors catalog 80 distinct GNN architectures designed to predict over 20 molecular properties across 48 datasets, addressing the rapid growth of this field. The work aims to consolidate the diverse approaches within AI-driven drug discovery to guide practical implementation.

**Key claims:**
- Graph neural networks are increasingly significant in drug discovery for molecular property prediction.
- Pharmaceutical companies are adopting GNNs for in-house drug discovery frameworks.
- This review classifies 80 GNN architectures for predicting more than 2.0 molecular properties using 48 different datasets.
- The review aims to structure the rapidly evolving field of AI-research in molecular property prediction.

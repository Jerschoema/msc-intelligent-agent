### 1. LLM-Mixer: Multiscale Mixing in LLMs for Time Series Forecasting
*Md Kowsher, Md. Shohanur Islam Sobuj, Nusrat Jahan Prottasha, E. Alejandro Alanis, Ozlem Ozmen Garibay, Niloofar Yousefi (2024)*

The paper introduces LLM-Mixer, a framework that integrates multiscale time-series decomposition with pre-trained LLMs for time series forecasting. It processes data at multiple temporal resolutions using downsampling and feeds these representations to a frozen LLM via a text prompt. The model excels in both short-term and long-term forecasting across diverse datasets, outperforming benchmarks like TIME-LLM, TimeMixer, and PatchTST. Notably, it demonstrates robustness on challenging datasets such as Traffic and Electricity.

**Key claims:**
- LLM-Mixer achieves competitive accuracy on short-term forecasting tasks across PEMS datasets, outperforming baseline models like TIME-LLM, TimeMixer, and PatchTST. (p. 5)
- LLM-Mixer demonstrates superior performance in univariate long-term forecasting, achieving the lowest MSE and MAE values across ETT benchmark datasets. (p. 5)
- Average pooling consistently yields a lower MSE across datasets, indicating its effectiveness for capturing multi-scale dependencies. (p. 7)
- The LLM-Mixer framework effectively captures both short- and long-term temporal dependencies, enhancing forecasting accuracy over existing methods. (p. 7)
